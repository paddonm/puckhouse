WATERLOO SISKINS

(1937 - Present)

The Waterloo Siskins are one of the oldest teams in Junior hockey. The team started out in the 1930's. They appear to have sat out from 1942-44 during World War II. In the 1950's, the Junior groupings in the Waterloo area evolved into the Big '10' by 1955. In 1956, Waterloo found themselves in the OHA Central Junior 'B'. In 1971, they left that league to join the Western Ontario Junior 'B' Hockey League. In 1973, the moved on once again to join the newly formed Southwestern Ontario Junior 'B' Hockey League. In 1974, this league became the Waterloo-Wellington Junior 'B' Hockey League. It changed its name again in 1977 when it became the OHA Midwestern Junior 'B' Hockey League. The Siskins remain a part of that league today.
