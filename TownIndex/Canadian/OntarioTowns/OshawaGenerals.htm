OSHAWA GENERALS

(1962 - Present)

The real birth of the Oshawa Generals was back in 1937 when they joined the Big 7 OHA Junior 'A' grouping replacing the Oshawa Majors as the towns top Junior team. The team was dominant in the early going winning Memorial Cups in 1939, 1940 and 1944. When they weren't winning, they were coming close losing in the finals in 1938, 1942 and 1943. The Generals fell back to the back in the 1950's. Things got very bad for the team in 1956 when their arena burnt down and they had to finish the season in nearby Bowmanville. Without an arena to play in, the Generals withdrew from the hockey scene in 1957.

The Generals were reborn in 1962 when their new arena was finally completed. The Generals were a top farm club of the Boston Bruins and the team joined the Metro Junior 'A' Hockey League. The league ended in 1963 and the Generals joined the Provincial Junior 'A' League. The Generals returned to the Memorial Cup final in 1966 lead by Bobby Orr. Unfortunately, they came up short against the western powerhouse, the Edmonton Oil Kings.

In 1981, the league changed its name to the OHL. The Generals lost in Memorial Cup finals in 1983 and 1987 before finally regaining the trophy when Eric Lindros lead the team to victory in 1990.
